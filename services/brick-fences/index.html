Why Choose a Brick Fence?

A brick fence is one of the most durable and visually appealing boundary options available to Brisbane homeowners. Built from clay bricks laid by experienced bricklayers, a brick fence offers superior strength, privacy, and noise reduction compared to timber or Colorbond fencing. It also adds significant street appeal and can increase the value of your property.

Brick fences are commonly used for front fences, boundary walls, and feature walls. They can be built as a single-face wall with one finished side, or a double-face wall where both sides are fully finished. Double-face brick fences are ideal for shared boundaries where the neighbour also sees the wall.

Brick Fence Types and Materials

The most common brick used in Brisbane is the standard 230mm x 110mm x 76mm clay brick. The wall thickness depends on whether the fence is single face or double face. Single face brick fences are typically 110mm thick, while double face fences are 230mm thick.

Brick fences can be built in a range of styles including:

  • Single face brick fence – one finished side, commonly used against boundaries where only the owner sees the face.
  • Double face brick fence – both sides finished, ideal for shared boundaries and premium street frontages.
  • Rendered brick fence – brickwork finished with cement render and paint for a modern, smooth appearance.
  • Brick fence with piers – vertical brick piers or columns spaced along the wall for strength and visual appeal.
  • Feature brick fence – decorative brick patterns, recessed panels, or contrasting brick colours.

Brick Fence Cost in Brisbane

Brick fence costs in Brisbane are typically calculated per square metre and depend on the brick type, wall height, footing requirements, pier spacing, and site access. As of 2026, single face brick fences generally range from $280 to $420 per square metre supply and install, while double face brick fences range from $380 to $520 per square metre.

Additional costs include concrete footings, pier construction, excavation, rendering, painting, and gate installation. A brick fence with a letterbox or mailbox built into the front fence will also add to the total cost.

Brick Fence Regulations in Brisbane

Before building a brick fence in Brisbane, it is important to check local council regulations. Generally, front fences are limited to 1.8 metres in height, and side and rear boundary fences can be up to 2.0 metres. Fences over these heights may require building approval.

Retaining walls over 1.0 metre in height often require engineering certification. If your fence is also retaining soil, you should consult a structural engineer or licenced builder to ensure compliance with the Queensland Building and Construction Commission (QBCC) standards.

How Long Does a Brick Fence Last?

With proper footings and quality workmanship, a brick fence can last 50 years or more. Clay bricks are resistant to termites, fire, and rot. The weakest points are usually the mortar joints, which may need repointing after 20 to 30 years depending on exposure to weather.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a brick fence cost in Brisbane?

Single face brick fences cost approximately $280 to $420 per square metre, while double face brick fences cost $380 to $520 per square metre. The total price depends on height, length, brick type, and extras such as piers and mailboxes.

How much does a brick fence cost per metre?

For a 1.8 metre high single face brick fence, expect $500 to $750 per linear metre. For a double face fence, $680 to $940 per linear metre.

Do I need council approval for a brick fence?

Fences under 1.8 metres front and 2.0 metres side/rear generally do not need approval in Brisbane. Higher fences, retaining walls, or fences near easements may require approval.

How long does it take to build a brick fence?

A typical 10 metre brick fence takes 2 to 4 days including footings, bricklaying, and cleanup. Larger or more complex fences may take longer.
