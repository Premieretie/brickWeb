Why Your Front Fence Matters

Your front fence is the first thing visitors and passers-by see. It defines your property boundary, provides security, and sets the tone for your home's street appeal. A well-designed brick or block front fence can increase property value and create a strong first impression.

Front fences in Brisbane are commonly built from brick, block, or a combination of masonry with timber or steel infill. Brick and block front fences are popular because they are durable, low maintenance, and can be matched to the style of the home.

Front Fence Styles

  • Solid brick front fence – full privacy, maximum noise reduction, premium street appeal.
  • Block front fence – modern, clean lines, can be rendered or painted.
  • Front fence with piers – solid fence panels between decorative masonry columns.
  • Front fence with infill – brick or block piers with timber, aluminium, or steel infill panels.
  • Rendered front fence – smooth cement render finish, painted in any colour.

Front Fence Cost Brisbane

Front fence costs in Brisbane vary based on materials, height, length, and extras such as gates, letterboxes, and lighting. As a guide, a 1.2 to 1.8 metre high brick or block front fence costs $280 to $520 per square metre.

A typical 10 metre front fence with a letterbox and gate can range from $5,000 to $12,000 depending on the finish. Use our front fence calculator to estimate your specific project.

Front Fence Regulations

In Brisbane, front fences are generally limited to 1.8 metres in height. Fences near intersections must also meet sight-line requirements. Always check with Brisbane City Council or your local authority before building.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a front fence cost in Brisbane?

A typical brick or block front fence costs $5,000 to $12,000 depending on length, height, materials, and extras.

How high can a front fence be?

Front fences in Brisbane are generally limited to 1.8 metres in height.

Do front fences need council approval?

Front fences under 1.8 metres usually do not need approval, but always confirm with your local council.

What is the best material for a front fence?

Brick and block are excellent for durability, security, and street appeal. Timber and steel are cheaper but require more maintenance.
