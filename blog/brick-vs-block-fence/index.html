Brick vs Block Fence: Which Is Better?

A detailed comparison for Brisbane and South East Queensland homeowners.

Choosing between a brick fence and a block fence is one of the most common decisions for homeowners in Brisbane. Both materials offer strength, privacy, and a premium finish, but they differ in cost, appearance, construction time, and maintenance. This guide helps you decide which option suits your home and budget.

Brick Fences

Brick fences are built from traditional clay bricks, usually 230mm x 110mm x 76mm. They offer a classic appearance, a wide range of colours, and excellent durability. Brick fences are ideal for homeowners who want a traditional or heritage look that matches their home.

Pros: Wide colour range, traditional appearance, long lifespan, high resale value.

Cons: Higher cost than block, longer construction time, fewer texture options unless rendered.

Block Fences

Block fences are built from concrete masonry units. They are larger than bricks, which makes them faster and cheaper to install. Block fences are available in smooth face, split face, and textured finishes, and they can be rendered or painted.

Pros: Lower cost, faster construction, modern appearance, strong when core-filled, good for retaining walls.

Cons: Limited natural colour range unless painted, can look industrial if not finished.

Cost Comparison

Brick fences typically cost $280 to $520 per square metre, while block fences cost $180 to $320 per square metre. Rendering a block fence can bring the price closer to a brick fence, but it still offers a more contemporary finish.

Which Is Better?

The best choice depends on your home style, budget, and purpose. Choose brick for a classic, timeless look. Choose block for a modern, cost-effective solution. For retaining walls, block is often the better engineering choice.
